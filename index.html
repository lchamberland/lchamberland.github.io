About me

I am a PhD student in the Agnarsson Lab at the University of Vermont. My research explores the interplay between long-distance dispersal and vicariance in the evolutionary history and biogeography of Deinopidae, the net-casting spiders. My research is part of an ongoing collaborative project, Caribbean Island Biogeography (CARBIO), aimed at uncovering why the Caribbean is a biodiversity hotspot. Currently, I am expanding the project globally to investigate the evolutionary and ecological shifts related to the complex visual sensory system of these spiders.

When I'm not nerding out about spiders, I'm racing mountain bikes across the Northeast.
